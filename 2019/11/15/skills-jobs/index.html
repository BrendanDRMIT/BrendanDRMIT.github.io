To bring the idea of automated street lighting to reality, I need to assemble a team of 4 experienced individuals. Each person would have the following skills, experience and degrees/certifications:

Programmers

I would probably have one experienced programmer on my team. He/she would need a lot of experience with various languages but especially Python and Java, as those languages are big players in the programming industry[1]. They will need to have a good understanding of how the GPS service works and have written a software that used the service before. A degree in IT or computer science is essential. The person will write the software that tracks vehicles and switch on the light accordingly. They will need to make sure that the new software will go well with the existing software that manages the lights.

Cybersecurity technician

We will need one person who have a degree in cybersecurity or something similar. That person needs to have a good knowledge of the language the programmers used to implement the service, and they will be responsible for testing and probing the programmersâ€™ code, making sure that they are secure to the highest standards so that it will be harder for attackers to break into and control the lights. He/she will also be responsible for ensuring that it work flawlessly and it does not contain mistakes or bugs.

Marketer

Last but not least, we will need someone to advertise and tell the world that Melbourne now has automated street lighting. He or she will be the person the news, magazines and other companies will interview. The marketer will need to have a good understanding of how the system works and if issues raise with the system, the manager will need to research it and report it to the rest of the team. He or she will need a degree in marketing, and have been involved in a large-scale project affecting the community before.

Electronic engineer

We will need a certified electrician or hardware designer to design and implement the hardware we will install into our lights. A degree in electronic engineering is mandatory. Experience with designing computer chips, programming them and embedding them into existing technology will be very useful. The engineer will work closely with the programmer, making sure that the hardware that will be designed, will work with the computer languages the programmers intend to use, and to make sure that it will blend smoothly into the existing infrastructure.
