Group Reflection

Overall, the team worked out above average. We continued to use Microsoft Teams, which was a very good online communication tool that served our purpose well. It also acted as our file sharing tool, which was very useful for sharing files and giving and receiving feedback. One thing that went well was how simple and quick it was to communicate. It was very easy to share files that was all located together in one place. Unfortunately some group members rarely communicated on the platform and one or two did not contribute at all, so there were more work for the members who were present. Another issue was that since it was very common for members to work at different times so that when one member writes something, then later goes offline, then another member shows up, dislikes what the other one wrote then updates it. The original member comes back later and he is like, “Oh, what happened to my text?”. We could have organised a frequent meeting so that we could have a good talk with every member of the team to discuss who is responsible for what, and provide others with feedback. 

This time around our team was much more organised in the beginning, one of our team members segmented each section of the assignment allocating a percentage of the total report for each task. This method of organising our individual workloads assisted us in ensuring a fair share of the work to be done and has made it easier to reflect on how each individual assisted the group. With this implemented our communications were set to a minimum, much less that in assessment number 2 with communications being limited to asking for help, clarification, or uploading our sections. This is both good and bad, it allowed us to get along with our jobs and become much more efficient, however, it also meant that we weren’t bouncing ideas off of each other potentially missing out on a broader range of ideas.  

The one thing that is learnt about groups, and especially in this situation we were in, being unable to communicate to each other face to face and to create bonds, is that, most of the times it is not an issue. A group can still function without the need to see each other as long as it is organised from the start. With this group a process was implemented in completing this assessment and even though there was a couple of little hiccups along the way, the process and assessment was completed. 

Comments on Audit Trail

This time around we were all already familiar with the format that we were going to use, considering Microsoft teams (MS teams) worked so well in the last assessment we decided to use it again. Before anyone commenced work Collin segmented the individual sections to be complete for assessment and allocated percentage points for each of them, we then all had a look over the document and come to an agreement on these percentage allocations. We used this document to self-allocate the sections which most interested us individually and were able to take advantage of  MS teams live cloud based word documents to ensure there was no overlap with expectations. From this point onward there was minimal contact, much less than we had last time, because we all knew our roles and expectations within the group, I think this worked well and allowed everyone to get on with their tasks. We did keep in contact with a couple of posts per week from each individual, most of these conversation were to; clarify data, seek approval, cooperate on sections, and confirm compliance with the criteria. I was surprised how smoothly everything ran this time, there were a couple of tasks which needed to be reallocated due to lifestyle or work changes which were posted in the group and almost immediately someone picked them up. Our audit trail in Microsoft teams should depict an organised, well managed team with all members chipping in to help complete the task. Unfortunately the same two members who didn’t make an effort in out last assessment still never showed in this one, however, we managed to get everything done none the less.
