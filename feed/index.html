The existing running cost for a streetlight is $0.21195/KWh, (Ergon Energy), with each motorway lamp being about 250 Watts, the electrical running cost per lamp is in the region of $232 per year, and on a suburban street this equates to about $88.00 per year this is with a 95-Watt lamp.

On a length of motorway there are about 22 streetlights per kilometre of road, whereas in suburban streets there are about 28 streetlamps per kilometre (Energyrating.gov). The resulting lighting cost to the government is estimated at $5104 and $2464 respectively, per year per kilometre of lit road. Although there are initiatives currently underway within the industry, specifically the Street Lighting Smart Controls program (SLSC) governed by the Institute of Public Works Engineering Australia (IPWEA), we don’t think that this is enough. These initiatives are majorly concerned with rolling out new LED lighting in streetlamps and too reduce costs with a lesser focus on the intelligent integration of the use of these streetlamps within society. The focus needs to be on the intelligent integration of communications devices to assist in the development of an intelligent system which not only reduces the environmental impact of our street lighting have, but also to improve the safety of drivers, and emergency services.

In this day and age communications devices make up so much of our day to day life and more importantly for our project, our driving experiences. Whether you’re following Google Maps on your way home, streaming music from Spotify, or on the phone with your loved ones; all of these services use some sort of frequency which

we can track and use to predict your actions. The primary use of our service would be to take advantage of live GPS updates and signals from streaming services or telephone towers from one’s communications devices to predict their movements along a path and pre-emptively light their way. This method of lighting will enable us to light only the roads which are under use and even specific sections providing the driver a radius of light which leads them through their journey. We expect this service will be able to cut our energy consumption on roads in half but that’s not all, we also expect to be able to integrate intelligence into the system which will enables us to gather raw data on how our roads are being used and where necessary make adjustments for this.

Should the system detect that a car may have stopped along its path or had a crash, our system will be able to detect this and communicate with the appropriate emergency services that an incident has happened, not only will this increase safety but also aid in returning the traffic to a smooth flow on our roads.

We expect that our system will be able to be retrofitted into the current services already available on roads today and take advantage of the current lighting infrastructure, maintaining low instillation costs. The service will also take advantage of pre-existing infrastructure such as local telephone towers, satellites, and other services to create a net of communications between road users and lighting.

Not only will our Smart system lower power consumption and reducing carbon emissions but by association it will also reduce government expenditure allowing funds to be spent more meaningfully on other government initiatives.

Motivation

The concerns of global warming are broad and severe with many harsh impacts “Over the past 50 years, the average global temperature has increased at the fastest rate in recorded history” All but one of the 16 hottest years in NASA’s 134-year record have occurred since 2000. (Macmillan, 2016). Macmillan also identifies the cause of global warming being that it is the collection of Co2 and other greenhouse gasses collecting in the atmosphere and trapping solar radiation within it. Our primary motivation for this project is to take one of the most power consuming government initiatives and transform it into an environmentally friendly version. We obviously cannot eliminate street lighting entirely and reducing the strength of light as this would increases risk to society, however, our intelligent system will severely reduce energy consumption and the greenhouse gasses released into the atmosphere, assisting in the fight against global warming. Not only will our system reduce power consumption, but it is possible we can eliminate it entirely with reduced energy usage it may be possible to affix solar panels to streetlights and abolish energy consumption of street lighting. The impact of this also results in huge savings for society either reducing taxes or allowing the government to reinvest the savings more efficiently.

Landscape

The landscape of street lighting in Australia has seen limited development in recent years, there are currently projects underway run by the IPWEA which aim to roll out new LED lighting to assist in reducing costs. The project aims to “reduce energy consumption by 30-70%+ and dramatic 50%+ reductions in maintenance costs” (IPWEA, 2019). We want to continue this development, our project will not require new lights to be installed as we will be building on the existing lighting infrastructure, rather we will be developing a software which intelligently

communicates with mobile devices and similar modules which can be fitted to cars for this purpose.

Our major competitor will be the SLSC project mentioned earlier, they have developed a system that has been rolled out before. The SLSC have a lot more experience and a better reputation than we do ourselves, the main point of difference for our project is that we have developed a system for manufacturing these devices extremely cheaply which does not require connection to a central mainframe, Rather the data stored by the devices will be able to pulled upon the regular maintenance of the lighting. The impact of this is lower costs however this restriction results in less capacity to assess live data from the lights.

]]> http://BrendanDRMIT.github.io/2019/11/15/overview/feed/ 0 137 Detailed Description http://BrendanDRMIT.github.io/2019/11/15/detailed-description/ http://BrendanDRMIT.github.io/2019/11/15/detailed-description/#respond Fri, 15 Nov 2019 02:08:12 +0000 http://BrendanDRMIT.github.io/?p=139 Aims 

The aim of this project is to develop a fully automated street lighting system to be used on major highways. This system would have the ability to identify a vehicle or vehicles location, adjust the lighting intensity accordingly for that area and remain on for a set distance in-front of the vehicle. It will reduce the total amount of time a light fitting will be on for thus reducing the running costs of the light and also reduce the total amount of light pollution emitted by the fitting. 

Goals 

1, Software coding: The software code will need to be capable of receiving information of surrounding ambient light, approaching vehicles, GPS tracking satellites, have the ability to read RFID tags, have the ability to communicate with other fittings within a local area and have the ability to be overridden at any time. 

2, Firmware: Firmware design and construction will be based on the software program. It will need to have the ability to receive information from various sources and to have switching outputs for the hardware. 

3, Hardware: The hardware design and construction must have the capability of being installed into the restricted areas of existing light fittings as the project must have the ability to be retrofitted. The hardware will need to be able to switch a 240 Volt AC supply, from a controlled input via the firmware. 

4, Production and installation: A limited number of the hardware will be produced and installed into test fittings this is to enable testing of the product in various weather conditions, testing by a sample public and resolving any bug issues. 

5, Mass production. 

The goals are set out in order of priority as any of the following goals cannot be achieved without completion of the previous goal. The most important part of the project is the software coding. The code and software design will be the most technical part of the project and when completed, would be considered to be proprietary software. 

Plans and Progress 

Background

When driving on the highway it was wondered why highway lighting was ‘on’ so far in the distance, especially when driving away from or to a populated area. Noticing that, the further away you get from a populated area, motorway lighting became non-existent. Of course, this was due to cost and requirements, the nearer you get to a city your speed is reduced, there is more of a build-up of traffic and turn offs need to be highlighted for a safe exit. Naturally the further you drive away from a city, your speed can increase, there is less traffic around you and turn offs may be 3,4, or 5, maybe more kilometers down the road. The need for street lighting in these areas are not required so much as these lengths of road are genuinely safer to drive on. 

So, street lighting is needed the nearer we get to a city, but if nobody is on the road at the time, there is no need for the streetlights to be ‘on’, and the further away we get from the city, streetlights are required even less. 

The need to have a streetlight ‘on’ at 2:00 AM when there is nobody on the road seems pointless and with the cost to run that light is being wasted. With the electrical cost to run one streetlight at about $200.00 per year with an average of the light being ‘on’ for 12 hours a day. (Ergon Energy Forecast Expenditure Summary, Street Lighting Services 2015 to 2020) state that their existing major street lighting asset base is a total of 48,763 units, if this is multiplied by the running cost of a light, the total cost of power to run the lights is in the region of $9,752,600 per year. Having lamps only come ‘on’ for only half that time could save in the region of $4,876,300 per year. Ergon Energy. With installation of energy efficient light-emitting diode (LED) lighting technology, this cost could be reduced even further. 

This is how and why this project began. 

What the project will do

The project will have the capability to enable a streetlight to function as an independent item and not as part of a bank of lights. It will be able to detect when a vehicle has passed a certain point on the road and if the light will need to be switched ‘on’. It will also be able to detect when a vehicle has passed a certain point which it will then switch ‘off’. The switching ‘on’ and ‘off’ will have the effect of creating a light corridor that the vehicle will be traveling in. The light itself would be able to communicate with the neighbouring light / lights to inform them that there is a vehicle approaching and to ‘hand over’ its knowledge. When this is completed and the vehicle has passed certain point, the light will dim to an ‘off’ status. The quantity of lights that would communicate with each other will depend on the speed of the vehicle, the faster the vehicle is going the more lights to be turned ‘on’ in front of the vehicle. 

How will it be done

The advantage of this project is that all streetlights would be able to be retrofitted with the technology and equipment required to make them become a smart light. I consider the equipment required would be a sensor to detect and register the speed of the vehicle, hardware for switching. The technology required will be for the communication protocol between the smart light and the ‘hand over’ of the light’s knowledge, with each luminaire having its own unique data address.  The mode of communication on a new installation will be CAT 7 cable as the information being transferred along the line would be minimal. With the speed of data transfer not being an issue, fiber optic cable will not be required. On fittings that are to be retro-fitted, Wi-Fi communication equipment would need to be installed. A method of Power Line Communication (PLC) will need to be investigated as a means of redundancy for the system. (Cypress Semiconductor, 2011). 

Stage of the Project

The project so far is in the Project Initiation stage and will need to have a feasibility study to be completed to see if it is a viable case. 

Changes to the Project

Changes to the initial project are to be an additional part of the project. The project will now need to include the capability for the smart lights to remain ‘on’ if there has been an accident, this will enable the emergency crews to work safely in that area. Emergency vehicles will need to have the ability to automatically override lights to ‘on’ when on route to an emergency and will need to be fitted with an RFID type tag.  With this, the technology to be installed within the lights will need to have the capability to receive information from an RFID tag, identify if the tag holder is on an emergency call and to adjust the state of the lights accordingly and it would also be able to remain ‘on’ until such a time that the ‘on’ state is no longer required. Inclusion of a light monitoring system to monitor lamp failure, loss of power, communications failure and system diagnostic. (Department of Planning, Transport and Infrastructure). 

Remaining Work

An application will need to be sent to the government to apply for a Community Energy Efficiency Program (CEEP) grant. LED lamp colour to be confirmed with local governments, as white in the colour range of 3500K to 5000K (Kelvin) is found to be better than sodium lights which are in the 3000K range. 

(Street lighting n.d.) 

Expected Results

Upon completion of this project the public would be able to see a noticeable reduction in light pollution, governments will have a greater cost saving in the running and maintenance of highway lighting with the savings being filtered down to the general public. The response time for the emergency services will reduce as the smart lighting will act as a guidance and light corridor for the emergency services. 

Roles 

The project requires specific roles to ensure successful completion. The following roles are essential to this project. 

Software Designer; Required to design and create the program for the controlling of the smart lights. The software designer collaborates with other IT specialists to deliver the software solution for the project. 

Project Manager; Required to manage the project, including resourcing specific skilled personnel to complete the project. A project manager is essential to ensure milestones and goals are achieved delivering the project on time and within budget. 

Hardware Engineer; Required for the installation and testing component of the project, including inspections and completing modifications that may be required. The hardware engineer will ensure all components meet the standards required. 

Programmer; Required to write the specific code for this project, enabling the smart lights to communicate. Coding is crucial for the project’s success. 

Lead Developer; Required for the responsibility of the overall planning and execution of the project. The lead developer will be the navigator of the team and to ensure all team members are on the same page. 

Scopes and Limits

The main goal of the project is to reduce costs spent on lighting each year. We hope to reduce by half, which will total in millions of dollars if you count the number of street lights in each state. One aim that will come with it is reduction of light pollution, which is blamed for “compromising health, disrupting ecosystems and spoiling aesthetic environments”[1]. If a vehicle has stopped at an unusual place, for example in the middle of the freeway, the GPS or some other sensors could have a “look” and if it is an accident or some other reason, it could alert the emergency services and keep the lights on until the freeway is completely cleared.

What we are NOT planning on implementing is doing a hardware upgrade. Rather than inserting sensors that track vehicles directly into the lights, which will take many hours and money to accomplish, we are only doing a software upgrade that uses existing technology such as cameras and GPS to accomplish the task. This will further reduce the costs of implementation. Another attractive idea that would be useful but we are not planning on adding is the for the opposite reason – such as if a criminal is speeding on the freeway, the lights, when they sense how fast he or she is driving, will deliberately turn off to make it harder for that person to run away. The person would be forced to slow down or crash.

Tools and Technologies

Software required for the project would be

– A programming language, most likely C/C++ or Java, and its compiler/interpreter. Examples include the GNU Compiler Collection(GCC) for C/C++ or the Oracle Java SE Development Kit for Java. We are not sure which language to implement the service in. We will need it to program the hardware we will be installing in each light to sense cars driving close by.  

– If one is available, probably a software simulator of streetlights will be useful for testing purposes too. We could apply the software controlling the lights and sensors into the emulation and we could observe the effects and fix and improve any issues. 

– Although highly unlikely, we may need a low-level hardware descriptor language like Verilog or VHDL for the electronic engineer to design the chips. 

A software license may be required for a streetlight simulator. 

Hardware required for the project: 

– A streetlight for experimentation so we can try installing the new sensors in it, and checking it to see of it works, and trying to find bugs and errors in the code. 

– Soldering irons, perfboards/stripboards/breadboards, resistors, diodes, capactitors, and various other small electronic parts and tools for the electronic engineer to make a prototype of and to test the sensor hardware which will go on the lights. 

– 3D printers will be a nice tool to have, but not necessary. 

– We will need sensors – motion sensors, light sensors, and cameras so that the lights have a good perspective of where the car is. 

People on the team who have experience with the tools and technologies described: 

– Michael has experience and a working knowledge of programming in C, C++ and Java with his interest in low-level computer programming. 

– As Colin is an electrician, he may have some experience in designing circuits and could possibly help in designing and implementing the sensors in the lights. 

– Brendan has experience with light coding, developing robots and using a 3D printer which can be helpful. He got all this experience from personal interest.

 Testing

The testing process will be over several stages and with different aspects of the project. Initially all testing will be completed in the mock-up stage, this being the testing of the software. 

The software testing will be, programming of the communication protocols, vehicle and speed detectors, configuring of the RFID detectors, on and off timers, contactor controllers, switching and the Wi-Fi protocol. This will then become the firmware for the lights. The next stage will be to join items with this firmware together to create a string. The string will then need to be programmed so as to follow the rules of switching, A set quantity of lights will be required to switch ‘on’ depending on speed of a vehicle also, the ability to override to the ‘on’ state if a RFID transmitter is detected (emergency stage) and to switch ‘off’ previous lights in the string. This stage will be considered successful if the lights can be switched ‘on’ by means of a passing object or triggered ‘on’ by a RFID transmitter. This stage of the testing will also be part of the feasibility study. 

The next test will be to install the firmware and hardware into several interconnecting streetlights over a track to confirm that the firmware and protocols are able to function under real life conditions. This will comprise of driving past the lights to see how they respond and testing in different weather conditions to confirm Wi-Fi communication. The final stage of testing will be to have members of the general public, drive pass the lights several times in the evening to see if they are ‘user friendly’. A report will be compiled from feedback to a questionnaire completed by the public involved in this test. 

Timeframe

For the first week, we will need to get everyone to be familiar with the project. Every member will have to read up the necessary materials and tools needed for this project. Weekly group meetings are going to be essential to be able to review the project as it is getting structured and be able to address any problems moving forward. For this for the second week, we will be analysing the tools and technologies needed for this project. We will also go over software and hardware requirements then make preparations for the coming development weeks. For development and design, we will make a 3-week timeline for this. With much preparation and knowledge, I think that we will be able to progress and make a working project in that timeframe. 7th week will be all testing and maintenance. We will make sure the project is working properly, assessing any errors and bugs if there are any, and address any functionality issues. For the last week, we will make our final report and documentation of the project.

Risks

The risks which may arise within the project could be, 

Software incompatibility with the hardware, there will be various hardware sensors installed within the fitting, using their own protocols which the software code will need to understand and act upon. 

Cost, the end cost of the item may be at such a level that it will not be a variable product to install into the fittings. 

Public perception, the general public may feel the product has a safety issue with the lights not being on all the time. 

Hacking, the product may be able to be hacked. 

Scope creep, the need to add more functions to the software from the original concept. 

Group Processes and Communications. 

The main form of communication for the group will be Microsoft Teams, this tool enables members to upload or download reports, and includes the ability to message either a single member or the group. The frequency of meetings will be a minimum of twice a week at the start of the project, increasing to three or four times a week nearing the completion date, if appropriate or required. 

If a group member is not responding to communication via Microsoft Teams, direct emails will be used as another form of communication to encourage group participation.

What went well?

]]> 80 Project Idea http://BrendanDRMIT.github.io/2019/10/10/project-idea/ Thu, 10 Oct 2019 06:26:58 +0000 http://BrendanDRMIT.github.io/?p=62 Overview

The project idea combines a passion for finance and I.T. to develop a program which allows the individual to easily create an investment portfolio which maximises their profitability whilst minimising risk for the individual. The program will use historic data from the Australian Stock Exchange (ASX) and apply statistical analysis to create an individual profile for each investment option, on top of this we will be experimenting with Artificial Intelligence (AI) to maximise efficiency and accuracy of outputs. The user will have complete control of their portfolio and receive live updated through a phone app which will notify them when their investments reach certain benchmarks of their portfolio goes outside of parameters.

Motivation

Investing is a very confusing and nerve-racking experience for someone who has never done it before, and it can often be very daunting for people, many not knowing where to start and as such spend hours upon hour researching where to best invest their money. Ultimately the decisions these people make are commonly the wrong ones for their circumstances resulting in an individual taking on more risk or having limited access to liquid funds. This program aims to fix these common issues by profiling the individual into risk profiles through means of a questionnaire.

Description

Upon signing up the user will be asked a series of questions based around their lifestyle, financial positions, goals etc. The answers to these questions will then profile the individual and create a dummy portfolio which outlines an ideal asset allocation for their position based on different asset classes (Stock, Bonds, property, cash). For example, someone who needs fast access to their cash will be recommended to hold a high percentage of their portfolio in cash or term deposits whereas someone who has a lot of disposable income or less of a need for liquidity will be recommended towards a higher percentage of stock or bonds. The individual will be able to alter their ideal portfolio if they disagree with the initial response from the programme, this allows for full control.

Once the user has set up their profile the programme will start recommending investments to build the users portfolio based on the asset allocations determined. Users will be able to access all securities available on the Australian Stock Exchange, however, the programme will have a built-in algorithm to determine their best choices. The user will be able to buy and sell the stock with a built-in, online broker program.

The underlying programme will pull data, in real time, directly from the Australian Stock Exchange, all data and charts commonly available will be displayed, however, an algorithm will assess data from the last 10 years to determine the standard deviation and based off of this a likely range (1, 2, and 3 standard deviations). Other information may be available based off of the AI component of the algorithm.

Once the user has set up their profile, they will be able to set up weekly or monthly payments into their account to help them budget for their investments. Each month the AI will suggest investment options based on their portfolio preference and the likely outcome of the investment.

With the large fluctuations of economic markets, it is easy for an individual’s portfolio to become unbalanced and this is why our project will involve updates and phone notifications to the user. Once an individual’s asset allocations move outside a certain range, the individual will be alerted that their profile has become too “Bullish” or too “Bearish” and recommend buying or selling stock based of rebalancing their portfolio.

A final AI component will be available, should the user wish to use it; this will give the program authority to buy and sell stock based off of indicators which would indicate to the computer that a stock is either going to crash of skyrocket, this could allow for instant gratification of small indicators which an individual might miss should they need to approve a trade. The user will also be able to set certain “sell points” for their stock to mitigate potential losses.

This program will allow people to live their daily lives without having to constantly check their booking app to see how their stock is doing or investing countless hours researching investment options.

Technical Requirements

In order to make this project work, we are going to need a number of IT and Finance professionals. A website professional will help us set up an efficiently running website with appropriate sales funnels and linking to the Australian Stock Exchange data, we will also need a highly secured database to store personal and account information. Finance professionals will be required along with an AI professional to assist in programming the algorithm which will assess stock and assist the user with their investment choices
