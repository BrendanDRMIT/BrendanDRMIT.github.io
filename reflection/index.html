Group Reflection

What went well? 

The whole group performed well with this assessment. There was communication between the team members and ideas were encouraged. Having a word document within Microsoft Teams with tasks to be completed, enabled everyone to modify and track which tasks needed to be completed and have ownership of tasks that they completed. 

What could be improved? 

The team itself, improved and bonded better over time, getting to know each team members strength and knowledge was a plus for the team but a better way to communicate between team members would be a great improvement. Perhaps the use of Github as opposed to Microsoft teams may have allowed for all team members to more easily access the service and reduced the work required in generating logs for the assessment, and to gain more knowledge and familiarity of Git. 

At least one thing that was surprising? 

The surprising part of this group was the way team members just assigned themselves a task and ran with it. Communication between members was, at times, found to be easy and helpful, at times finding it surprisingly easy to have just a chat.  I was surprised with the functionality of MS Teams and how easy it made communications between team members as I had never used this service before. 

At least one thing you have learned about groups. 

One thing that has been learnt about this group is their willingness to contribute, knowing that each member wants to achieve the best possible result for the group as a whole. A second thing learnt would be how easily groups flow when there is a more systematic approach and everyone knows what they should be doing, or what others are already doing.  

Tools.  

The communication log between each member using Microsoft Teams enabled everyone to see and check their assigned tasks and the progress of other team members. This ensured that a task was not completed twice by other members and that each member knew what the other members were completing. We also used Wordpress to design and implement our website. All the work uploaded to MS Teams were then ported to the website.
